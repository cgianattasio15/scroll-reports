March Direction

Strategy Adjustments

Three high-priority moves for March — grounded in February’s data and mapped to DEFINE Oakley’s core business goals.

1
Doubling Down

Make gym-culture, relatable Reels a monthly anchor

Reach & Views · Awareness Goal

Why

The Feb 19 punctuality Reel (4,745 views, 8 comments, 6.1s watch time) outperformed every other post by 4×. It worked because it validated a shared experience that every Define member has had — a relatable, opinion-led moment that made people feel seen. That’s the format that earns algorithmic distribution for this account.

↗

What This Moves

Views above 60K → Reach/Day above 1,500 floor → New Followers above 100 floor → more first-timers entering the booking funnel.

2
Needs Fixing

Rebuild saves with educational & reference-worthy content

Saves & Watch Time · Engagement Goal

Why

Saves collapsed from 36 in January to 3 in February — a 91.7% drop. February’s content mix was almost entirely motivational and community-focused, which doesn’t drive saves. Saves require content people want to reference: first-class guides, class format breakdowns, what to bring, childcare logistics, or “what to expect” content.

↗

What This Moves

Saves above 80 floor → improved Watch Time and Retention as structured content holds attention longer → stronger warm audience signal for the algorithm.

3
Testing Next

Test direct booking page as bio link destination

Link Taps & PCR · Conversion Goal

Why

PCR is 5.3% — roughly 1 in 20 profile visitors taps the link. The current bio link is Linktree, which adds a click layer before the booking page. Replacing it with a direct booking link removes friction from the conversion path. CTR improved to 4.1% in February, confirming people are visiting the profile — the gap is converting that visit to a booking click.

↗

What This Moves

PCR above 10% floor → Link Taps above 30 floor → measurable increase in first-class bookings from organic Instagram traffic.
