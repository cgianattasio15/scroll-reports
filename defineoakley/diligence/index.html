From a personal Instagram with no analytics infrastructure to a top-of-class service-business account, under continuous Scroll Media management since February 2024.

01 · BEFORE
January 2024

A studio account without the infrastructure to measure itself.

Heading into 2024, DEFINE Oakley's Instagram was managed in-house alongside the day-to-day of running a studio. The account was not yet a Meta Business profile, which meant no Insights, no daily reach data, and no audience or content analytics retained at the platform level. The first 90 days of Meta-direct performance history in this report don't exist. Not because they weren't happening. Because no one was capturing them.

02 · THE BRIEF
February 2024

Take social off the owner's plate without losing the brand voice.

DEFINE Oakley engaged Scroll Media on February 5, 2024. The owner was spending more time on content and channel management than the business could justify, with no formal social strategy in place. The ask was for a partner who could match the studio's brand, tone, and aesthetic closely enough to operate the account end-to-end on her behalf. The first three months were foundational: Business profile activation on April 30, 2024 (the moment Meta Insights began recording data), content cadence calibration, and audience baseline establishment. Performance data from this onboarding window is not retained at the platform level by design. The work was the setup itself.

02 Performance methodology

The analytical framework. Each metric maps to a stage of the Instagram funnel and a maturity stage based on follower count.

Stage targets are taken from a 2025 calibration of Instagram benchmarks for service businesses.

Top of funnel

Awareness

Reach, views, and impressions. How many unique accounts encounter the brand on Instagram, and how often. The upstream pool from which engagement and conversion are drawn.

Middle of funnel

Engagement

Saves, shares, comments, retention rate, and watch time. Signals of audience intent and content quality. Saves and shares are the two metrics Instagram's 2025 algorithm weights most heavily for organic distribution.

Bottom of funnel

Conversion

Profile visits, click-through rate, and link taps. The bridge from Instagram to revenue actions. Service-business benchmarks differ from CPG and retail and are calibrated separately.

Maturity stages

100K to 500K followers

Each stage maps to its own metric target ranges. Performance is graded against the targets for the active stage at month end. The three status labels used throughout this report are EXCEEDING above the upper target, ON TRACK within the target range, and WATCH below the lower target.

08 Methodology and data scope

Full disclosure of data sources, retention boundaries, scoring methodology, and known limitations.

This section is the basis for any independent verification of the report.

Data sources

This report draws from two independent data sources. Meta direct (via Meta Business Suite) is the primary source for daily account-level metrics: reach, profile visits, link clicks, and content interactions. Metricool, a third-party social analytics platform pulling from the official Instagram Graph API, is the primary source for per-content lifetime data (reels, posts, stories) and daily follower count. Secondary references: Instagram-published 2025 industry benchmarks for service-business engagement rates (SocialInsider, Dash Social) for context where labeled.

Cross-validation: Meta and Metricool reach for the seven-month overlap period (October 2025 to April 2026) match within 6.7%, within standard third-party platform tolerance. The agreement between two independent sources is the data integrity check on every figure in this report.

Why the data layers differ in granularity

Three layers of data feed this report and each has its own retention boundary. Meta retains daily account-level metrics (reach, profile visits, link clicks) from the date the account became a Business profile, April 30, 2024 in DEFINE Oakley's case. That gives 24 months of authoritative daily-granularity Meta-direct data and forms the spine of the multi-year reach trajectory in Section 6. Metricool, the third-party tool, captures everything Meta provides plus per-content lifetime metrics and daily follower count, but only from the date the brand was added to Metricool, October 1, 2025. That gives a deeper seven-month window for content-level analysis. Instagram itself retains reel-level insights for the lifetime of each reel, which is why reel data extends back to May 2024 across both sources.

The account has been under continuous Scroll Media management since February 5, 2024, a 27-month track record at the time of this report. The first three months of the engagement (February to April 2024) precede the Business profile activation; performance data from that onboarding window is not retained at the platform level by design.

Scoring methodology

Each month receives a composite score on the 6.0 to 10.0 scale. The score reflects performance against Lift Stage targets across eight evaluated metrics. Metrics are organized in three weighted bands: quality and conversion signals (saves, shares, retention rate, click-through rate, profile conversion rate) carry the highest weight, volume and distribution signals (reach, follower change, views, profile visits, link taps) carry standard weight, and context signals (comments, average watch time) carry lower weight.

The specific weighting coefficients used to combine these statuses into the final score are proprietary to the methodology. The output of the framework, the numeric score and the badge counts, is presented in full and is auditable against the underlying data. Existing scores for January, February, and March 2026 (7.5, 7.5, 8.2) were issued in the live monthly client reporting cycle and are reproduced unchanged. Scores for October, November, and December 2025 are retroactive applications using the same framework. The April 2026 score is preliminary.

Known limitations

Conversion-stage metrics (click-through rate from profile, profile visits, link taps, profile conversion rate) are not included in the trajectory charts because they were not captured by the third-party platform prior to October 2025 and are not retained historically by Instagram. The seven-month detailed window does include these metrics in the live monthly reporting cycle and they are available on request.

Reach values reported at the daily and monthly aggregate level reflect the sum of reach attributed to content published in the period, not unique-account reach across the account. Where the report distinguishes, it does so explicitly.

Saves and shares figures rely on Metricool's pull from the Instagram Graph API. Minor lag and discrepancy versus native Instagram Insights is normal at the daily level. Aggregate weekly and monthly figures match within typical industry tolerance, under 2%.

Independent verification

The raw daily-granularity data underlying this report is provided alongside it in two files. define_oakley_daily_meta.csv contains 731 days of Meta-direct daily metrics from April 30, 2024 to April 30, 2026. define_oakley_daily.csv contains 213 days of Metricool detailed metrics for the seven-month measurement window (October 2025 to April 2026). Any figure presented in this document can be independently verified by aggregating either file. The post-level lifetime data (188 reels, 22 posts) is provided in monthly_aggregates.json. Top content URLs in Section 7 have been independently verified against the live @defineoakley account; the audit record is in handoff/05_url_verification.json.
