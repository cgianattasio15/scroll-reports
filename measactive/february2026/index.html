Analysis

What We Learned

Key signals, wins, and gaps from February that inform March’s strategy.

Signal

January Was an Event-Driven Peak — February Is the Baseline

January’s 8.6 score was driven by the Galentine’s Brunch event, which created a temporary spike in reach, followers, and link taps. February’s 7.5 is the normalized baseline. The strategic implication: MEAS needs consistent monthly events (Run Club, seasonal launches, community events) to sustain the January-level performance, not just one annual event.

Win

Watch Time & Retention Are Exceeding — Content Quality Is Strong

Watch Time at 5.25s and Retention at 49% are both exceeding Lift targets. The content is holding attention at a strong rate. This is the foundation to build on: as reach scales in March, the high retention rate will compound into stronger follower acquisition and engagement.

Watch

Saves Are Critically Low — Reference Content Is Missing

Saves at 3 are well below the 60 Lift floor. The activewear vertical has a clear save-driving format: outfit inspiration, product reference lists, and “what to wear for X” content. Adding explicit “Save this for your next order” CTAs and creating monthly outfit inspiration carousels are the direct levers to move this metric above floor.

Signal

Spring Running Season Is the Next Event Opportunity

The Feb 26 “Winter running broke me. But listen… we are so close. Spring running season” Reel (9.42% engagement) is the clearest signal of what March needs to be. The Spring Running Season is the next event-driven content opportunity to rebuild reach above the 1,500 floor and drive new follower acquisition through the Run Club format.
