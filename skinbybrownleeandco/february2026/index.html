Analysis

What We Learned

Key signals, wins, and gaps from February that inform March’s strategy.

Win

Perimenopause Series Is the Account’s Strongest Differentiator

The perimenopause and hysterectomy content series drove the top 2 posts of the month by engagement and the highest saves (18). No other esthetician account is creating this content at this level of specificity and personal vulnerability. This is the moat. Scaling this series is the single highest-leverage content decision for March.

Win

CTR & PCR Confirm Exceptional Booking Funnel Quality

CTR at 13.01% (2.6x ceiling) and PCR at 15.6% (exceeding ceiling) confirm that the content-to-booking funnel is working at an exceptional rate. The audience that sees this content is highly motivated to book. The constraint is reach volume, not conversion quality.

Watch

Retention % Dropped to 24% — Hooks Need Structural Fix

Retention dropped from 39% to 24% — a significant decline. The Reels are losing viewers before the end. The fix: Add a clear “here’s what you’ll learn” promise in the first 2 seconds of every Reel, and use numbered steps to create a completion incentive. The perimenopause series is the highest-priority format to apply this fix.

Signal

Virtual Client Content Is Expanding the Geographic Reach

The Feb 23 Virtual Client from Japan Carousel (7.17% engagement) confirms that Brownlee’s expertise is attracting clients beyond the local market. This is a significant business opportunity: virtual consultations and product recommendations can scale without geographic constraints. This content pillar should be developed into a consistent monthly series.
