Analysis

What We Learned

Key signals, wins, and gaps from February that inform March’s strategy.

Win

Founder Story Content Is the Breakout Format

The “Nineteen years ago” post drove 15,157 views and 104 comments at 10.45% engagement — the best performance in the account’s history. The “18 years ago — Angela” post drove 8.81% engagement. The pattern is clear: staff origin stories that connect the community to Up & Running’s 19-year history are the account’s highest-leverage content format. Every staff member’s story is a potential breakout post.

Win

New Brand Launches Are the Shares Engine

The Salomon launch Carousel drove 66 shares and 15.6% engagement — the highest of any content type. New brand/product launches are the shares engine for Up & Running. The audience shares new product announcements with their running partners. Every new brand launch should follow the Salomon Carousel format with an explicit “Share with your running partner” CTA.

Watch

High Reach Not Converting to New Followers

Avg Reach/Day exceeded the Spark ceiling at 1,772/day, but New Followers only grew by 27 — below the 40 floor. The founder story content is reaching existing followers’ networks but not converting them to followers. Adding explicit Follow CTAs (“Follow us for more Running 101 content”) to the high-reach posts is the direct fix.

Signal

Account Is Approaching Lift Stage Readiness

Total Views at 35,614 are approaching the Spark ceiling of 40,000. Reach/Day exceeded the Spark ceiling. Watch Time and Retention both exceeding targets. Up & Running is showing Lift-stage performance signals in Month 4 of Spark. If March sustains this trajectory, the account may be ready for a stage review in April.
