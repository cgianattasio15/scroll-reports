February Direction

Strategy Adjustments

Three high-priority moves for February — grounded in January data and mapped to your core business goals.

1
Doubling Down

Scale community challenge and event content as the reach engine

Avg Reach/Day · Awareness Goal

Why

The Frozen Feet Challenge drove 7,226 views — 46% of January’s total from a single post — and confirmed that community challenge content is the account’s primary organic distribution format. No other content type came close. Scaling this formula with February event content and challenge follow-ups is the direct lever to push Avg Reach/Day above the 300 floor.

↗

What This Moves

Avg Reach/Day toward the 300 floor → more new eyes on the account → accelerated follower growth and event attendance.

2
Testing Next

Test educational shoe comparison content with explicit Save CTAs

Saves · Conversion Goal

Why

Saves are at 1 against a 10 floor — the largest gap metric in January. Educational shoe comparison content (“Stability vs. neutral — which one do you actually need?”) with “Save this before your next shoe fitting” CTAs is the highest-probability format to drive Saves. This content type provides reference value that people return to, building a warm audience of high-intent buyers.

↗

What This Moves

Saves toward the 10 floor → stronger content utility signals → a growing library of warm, high-intent savers who return for a fitting.

3
Fixing

Add an explicit CTA to every post — no more CTA-free content

Comments, Saves, Followers · All Goals

Why

The Frozen Feet Challenge drove 7,226 views with 0 comments and 0 shares. High reach with zero engagement depth is a CTA problem. Every post needs at least one explicit CTA — Save, Comment, Share, Follow, or Book. Starting with the Frozen Feet Challenge recap in February, every post should have a specific action for the viewer to take.

↗

What This Moves

Comments above the 15 floor → Saves toward target → Link Taps sustained above 5 → more qualified fit booking inquiries from content that drives action.
