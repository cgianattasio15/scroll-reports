Analysis

What We Learned

Key signals, wins, and gaps from February that inform March’s strategy.

Win

CTR Remains Exceptional — Content Quality Is Not the Problem

CTR at 18.1% is 3x the Lift ceiling. The audience that sees Lane & Kate content is highly motivated to visit the profile. This confirms that the content strategy is working — the growth constraint is reach volume, not content quality. The account needs more distribution, not better content.

Win

Trend-Forward Content Is the Shares Engine

The Feb 26 “Talking trends” Reel drove 112 shares — more than the rest of the month combined. The pattern is clear: trend-forward content with a custom consultation angle activates the share reflex. This format should be a consistent monthly pillar, not an occasional post.

Watch

Retention % Dropped Significantly — Needs Investigation

Retention dropped from 30.49% in January to 16% in February. This is a significant decline that requires investigation. Either the Reel content is losing viewers early, or the metric calculation changed. Structured tutorial-format Reels with numbered steps are the fix to improve completion rates above 50%.

Signal

Education Content Drives Saves — A Durable Advantage

The Feb 19 Jewelry Care Reel drove 21 saves — the highest of the month. The audience is bookmarking Lane & Kate as a jewelry reference resource. This is a durable competitive advantage: educational content that helps people care for their jewelry builds long-term loyalty and positions the brand as the authority in the space.

March Direction

Strategy Adjustments

Three high-priority moves for March — grounded in February’s data and mapped to your core business goals.

1
Doubling Down

Make trend-forward Reels a monthly non-negotiable

Reach & Shares · Awareness Goal

Why

The Feb 26 “Talking trends” Reel drove 112 shares — more than every other post combined. That single post confirmed that trend-forward content with a “we have more ideas” angle is the primary organic reach driver for this account. One post shouldn’t be an outlier; it should be a repeatable pillar.

↗

What This Moves

Shares above the 60 floor → expanded organic reach → more profile visits and DM inquiries from warm, consultation-ready audiences.

2
Doubling Down

Keep education content as a consistent monthly pillar

Saves & Authority · Community Goal

Why

The Feb 19 Jewelry Care Reel drove 21 saves — the highest of the month. The audience is bookmarking Lane & Kate as a jewelry reference resource. That’s a trust signal that compounds: saved content gets revisited, shared with partners, and converts to consultations over time.

↗

What This Moves

Saves toward the 80 floor → stronger content utility signals → positions L&K as the go-to jewelry authority for high-intent buyers.

3
Fixing

Restructure Reels with numbered steps to stop early drop-off

Retention & Watch Time · Engagement Goal

Why

Retention dropped from 30.49% in January to 16% in February — viewers are leaving Reels early. Numbered steps (“Step 1… Step 2…”) create a completion incentive that keeps viewers watching. Tutorial-format Reels (ring care, stone selection, sizing guides) are naturally suited to this structure.

↗

What This Moves

Retention back toward 30%+ → Watch Time above the 6s floor → stronger algorithmic distribution for all Reel content.
